This is another attempt to help alleviate many of the frustrations players are having vs specific units, cries of p2w, and the slow pace of WC and unit acquisition.

Overall, I think the starter deck is solid. It provides a decent foundation and introduction to the game, without demanding too much from the player, all whilst giving them different options on how to approach a match. It's not perfect, but a few pushes in the right direction could really improve the initial player experience.

The following units are great, and often used in high-end decks:
- **Crab**
- **King Crab**
- **Heavy Ballista**
- **Airship**

These units are often replaced or outshined by other units, but aren't inherently a problem:
**- Hunter** - very outshined by Recall Hunter, and maybe even Beetle to be honest. The biggest culprit is Beetle, being a similar, non-ability a-move style unit.
**- Bulwark** - Having a tanky versatile air unit is unique, but it doesn't help that it's so big and slow. Going "mass air" when paired with Butterflies just doesn't work well, but this is more to do with Butterfly's issues. Having the below suggested unit could help this issue.

These units could use some fixes:
- **Ballista** is probably the worst unit in the game. It's overshadowed by every one of the other t2 splash units, especially shocker, and even King Crab from the same deck! It's slow, doesn't deal much damage, and splash range is small. It needs a unique identity, a few buffs to make the unit stand out amongst the rest to be an attractive choice. Some simple improvements would be: a larger splash area, or longer range.
- **Butterfly** is another questionable starter deck unit. After playing with it for awhile, I don't think it's a bad unit at all, but it might not be a good choice for the starter deck. It trades unfavorably versus tier 1 AA, and given this deck is aimed for beginners, it simply requires too much care and attention to be effective.

Also, the starter deck is having issues versus some more popular units, namely **Mortar** and **Destroyer.** King Crab, the supposedly durable unit for the starter deck, is one shot by destroyers, rendering them almost worthless. King Crab also has low health (lowest of all durable units) that it's difficult to deal with a large mass of mortars. This can make the game feel unfair to those who don't have access to more suitable counters like Crusader, Mammoth or Sniper. King Crab might need to be re-worked slightly, but it already has its place as the only durable splash unit in the game.

The starter deck also struggles versus heavy durable unit spam, as both of the foundry units are splash, and there isn't a good option to fight against them. Sure, players could go Butterfly, but even a small squad of t1-AA will destroy them.

A common complaint is how the game locks you into using the starter deck + free unit rotation at the very beginning. I think if Northern Performance had a few more units unlocked through the proving grounds, it would give players more agency over their deck construction and play style. I'm suggesting at least three new units be added, each with a similar philosophy to the current units: simple to use, solid, flexible units.

- **A ranged core unit.** Something like a Gunbot without Overclock. This gives players the option of going melee or ranged from early on and decide if they like a particular play style. It additionally provides synergies with King Crab, wanting to pair a durable melee unit to front-line when paired with this unit. Also helps hedge against heavy durable units, as ranged units have much more usable surface area to deal damage. Users should start with Crab and have the option to swap to this later.
- **A "fighting" anti-ground (or versatile) air unit, built from Starforge.** Butterfly works better as a harassment unit then an army fighting unit, yet it isn't listed so. This could be a new unit similar to Dragonfly or Falcon. Beginners need access to a simpler, more forgiving unit. One which doesn't trade badly versus t1 AA. Let this new unit be the default, and butterfly be an unlockable later. That way, players can opt into either a death ball or harassment based style for air.

For Foundry, there are a few suggestions, and either one would be great. Both of them are single-target focused, since we already have access to two splash units in the deck:
- **A single target, burst damage unit.** Something similar to destroyer to help out versus the above mentioned durable unit spam decks. Since the King Crab and Ballista are available, this gives starter decks an option to include both splash and anti-durable units for a stronger t2 mid game. Honestly it would be cool to have a melee-burst damage unit, but I'll leave that up to the designers.
- **A longer range, single target damage unit**. Something similar to sniper to help out versus mortars, and also to have another long range unit available at Foundry t2 tech. Also a non-splash unit, since the starter deck provides a choice between King Crab or Ballista for splash. This would also open up a "turtle and harass" style since starter deck could have both butterflies and this unit. Doesn't need to necessarily out-range mortars, but something big and chonky which gives the starter deck a better chance.

In general though, the North Performance starter deck is quite good, and against most decks is viable at all levels of play. Giving players access to a few more simple starter units would alleviate many of the issues new accounts and player suffer, and further open up the core gameplay loop of deck building from the very beginning. Even 3 new starter units (without factoring in the free rotation), creates something like 18 different possible decks, compared to the single deck available now.
